I’m Josh Kaplowitz, a nuclear-trained U.S. Naval submarine officer with a computer science background and a track record of operating in high-consequence environments.

I started as a garbage man initially, enlisted in the Navy in 2015, worked on the USS GERALD R. FORD maintaining mission-critical networks, and earned an appointment to the U.S. Naval Academy where I studied Computer Science (Class of 2022).

Since then I’ve worked inside Naval Reactors in Washington, DC, completed the Navy's intense nuclear training program, and now lead technical teams on a nuclear-powered submarine. Outside the Navy, I’ve built and launched products, raised early capital, and pitched senior leaders. I’m focused on hard tech (energy, manufacturing, and frontier systems) and I’m building toward a post-Navy transition into launching my own company.

Defense / GovTech

JUNTO — funded Navy program

JUNTO started as a product idea, then became a real program. I built the application, pitched it to senior Navy leadership (including the Undersecretary of the Navy and the CNO in 2020), secured $569K in initial funding, and helped drive it into a million-dollar project.

I learned a lot working on this project and was almost kicked out of the Naval Academy for it. Over the next 2-3 years I led an entire software team based out of Georgia Tech. It was extremely painful to work within the confines of the government procurement rules. When we ran out of money, the team was legally not allowed to work on the app until new funding came which could take a month or more. The project was turned over to someone else and I realized the next project I was going to build was going to be in the private sector.

Trajectory and Ethos

Leave Navy → full-time build

Plan: finish my Navy tour strong, compound leverage (technical depth, credibility, relationships), and build an industrial company that meaningfully advances civilization. I’m not interested in novelty apps or marginal conveniences.

I’m focused on physical technologies (energy, manufacturing, and frontier systems) that expand what humanity can do and where it can go. I didn’t start with advantages. I believe leadership is earned through competence, judgment, and long-term thinking. I know I can do more, and I am building toward becoming a world-class leader in technology and industry.
